Article content The police service on Friday said a male suspect handed a local restaurant clerk an odd-looking $10 bill to pay for an order. When the clerk noted the bill looked strange and told the man they’d have to examine it more closely to determine its authenticity, the man immediately left without his order, the $10 bill or any change. LaSalle police warn businesses of suspect with counterfeit U.S. cash Back to video The man reportedly had a large stack of other U.S. currency on hand. A LaSalle police officer attended the restaurant and confirmed the bill was a poor-quality counterfeit utilizing the wrong kind of paper. It was also missing several standard security features, including colour-shifting ink, watermarks, symbols of freedom, microprinting and proper colouring. The bill itself was not cleanly cut, and the image on its reverse side was facing the wrong way, police said.
